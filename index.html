TOSS: Temporal Offset Supervision for Low-Bitrate Streaming Audio Codecs on Mobile Devices.

Recent neural audio codecs achieve high perceptual quality at low bitrates, but fully causal streaming codecs still suffer from the lack of future acoustic context. We introduce a low-bitrate streaming codec family for mobile devices, supporting 1.875-6.25 kbps, 24-32 ms algorithmic latency, and 2.8M-18.6M parameters. Temporal Offset Supervision aligns reconstruction with a temporally shifted target, improving causal reconstruction while preserving a competitive quality-latency-bitrate trade-off.

Method

Delayed causal reconstruction without architectural lookahead.

TOSS changes the temporal alignment of the training objective rather than the codec architecture. The encoder and decoder remain strictly causal, while a specified output delay D_T lets the model learn a better delay-quality operating point.

01

Temporal Offset Supervision

Supervise x_hat[t + D_T] with x[t]; D_T=0 recovers ordinary sample-aligned training.

02

Strictly causal streaming

All convolutional operations use only current and past samples, with chunk-wise state caching and overlap-add.

03

Scalable RVQ bitrate

With K=1024, fs=16 kHz, and hop size H=256, each active quantizer contributes 0.625 kbps.

04

Mobile-oriented model family

Tiny, Small, and Base cover 2.81M, 4.83M, and 18.6M parameter operating points.

Causal encoder, RVQ, causal decoder, TOSS alignment.
